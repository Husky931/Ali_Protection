# Alibaba Scam Reports — Landing Page Brief

## What this is
A community-driven, public database of fraud reports about scam sellers on Alibaba.com. Buyers who got burned submit a report; an admin reviews and approves it; the report becomes a public, SEO-indexed page. The goal is that when someone Googles a seller's name before placing an order, our report shows up and warns them.

## The problem
Alibaba shields its sellers. Bad reviews and 1-star ratings often don't surface in the overall seller score, and the Trade Assurance policy rarely helps once a seller stops replying. Buyers — especially first-time importers and small businesses — have no neutral place to check whether a seller has scammed others. They wire money, receive junk (or nothing), and the story dies in a private chat.

## Who it's for
- Small business owners and resellers sourcing products from Alibaba
- Hobbyists and one-time buyers placing their first overseas order
- People who have already been scammed and want to warn others / vent / be heard

Tertiary: journalists, anti-fraud researchers, people doing due diligence.

## What the site does
1. **Submit a report** — anonymous form: seller name, Alibaba seller URL, product, quantity, total paid, currency, industry, and the full story of what happened.
2. **Moderation** — every report is reviewed by an admin before it goes public. Nothing is published unsanitized.
3. **Browse & search** — homepage lists approved reports with a search box (by seller, product, keyword).
4. **Per-report pages** — each report lives at its own URL with the seller's name in the slug, optimized to rank for "[seller name] scam" and "[product] alibaba review" queries.

## Voice and tone
Empathetic, plainspoken, slightly angry on behalf of the user. Not corporate, not legalistic, not clickbaity. Written by someone who's been scammed and wants to help others avoid it. Trust and authenticity matter more than polish — this is a community wall, not a marketplace.

Avoid: hype, "revolutionary," "AI-powered," fake urgency, dark patterns, anything that smells like a coupon site.

## Key messages (in priority order)
1. **You're not alone, and your story can protect the next buyer.**
2. **Alibaba protects sellers. We protect buyers.**
3. **Anonymous, free, moderated — no accounts, no spam.**
4. **Search any seller before you wire money.**

## Hero — required ingredients
- **Headline**: "The place on the web to report a bad Alibaba deal." (the word *Alibaba* should pop visually)
- **Subhead**: "Share your story. Protect others from fraudulent sellers."
- **Primary CTA**: "Share Your Story" → goes to the report form
- **Secondary CTA**: "Browse Reports" → scrolls to the community feed
- A hero image/illustration showing how Alibaba's UI hides bad ratings would reinforce the thesis (the current site uses a screenshot for this).

## Sections the landing page should contain
1. **Hero** (above)
2. **Why this site exists** — a short, first-person story: "I got scammed on Alibaba. Here's what happened, and why I built this." Honest, 3–4 short paragraphs.
3. **How it works** — 3 steps: *Submit → Moderated → Goes public and indexed.* Reassures users that reports aren't a free-for-all.
4. **Search / Browse community reports** — visible card list of recent reports, each showing seller name, product, amount lost, and a snippet. Search box at the top.
5. **Report a seller** CTA strip — large, hard to miss, repeated near the bottom.
6. **Footer** — minimal: about, contact, a small disclaimer that reports represent users' experiences.

A floating "Report a seller" button persists on every page.

## What a report contains (data model, for reference)
- Seller name + Alibaba seller URL
- Product name + product URL
- Quantity ordered
- Total paid + currency
- Industry / category (Electronics, Textiles, Machinery, etc.)
- Free-text story of what happened
- Created date, slug, status (pending / approved / rejected)

Reports are anonymous — there are no user accounts.

## Trust & safety cues to surface in the design
- "rate-limited submissions"
- A clear note that the site reflects user experiences and is not affiliated with Alibaba.

## Brand & visual direction
- **Palette**: warm beige background (#f5f3ef), near-black ink text (#121212), muted gray secondary text (#6b7280), **orange** as the accent / alarm color (think construction-cone orange, not pumpkin-spice). Soft orange-50 gradients in hero backgrounds.
- **Mood**: warm, human, slightly imperfect — closer to a community bulletin board than a SaaS site. Not dark mode, not neon, not glassmorphism.
- **Type**: clean humanist sans-serif (system stack works fine — Helvetica Neue / Inter feel).
- **Shapes**: rounded corners (lg / 2xl), soft shadows, generous padding. Cards over tables.
- **Imagery**: real screenshots of Alibaba's UI > stock illustrations. Anything that feels like a McKinsey deck is wrong.

## Non-goals (please don't add)
- User accounts / login
- Paywall, pricing tiers, "Pro" plan (a $3 paywall is a *future* idea, not landing-page material)
- Forums, comments, upvotes
- Generic "trust badges" from invented certifications
- Newsletter signup as the primary CTA — the primary CTA is always *submit a report* or *browse reports*

## SEO is the growth channel
Every section should read naturally to a human AND include the phrases buyers actually search: "Alibaba scam," "Alibaba seller scam," "[seller name] review," "got scammed on Alibaba," "Alibaba Trade Assurance not working." Don't keyword-stuff — just don't avoid the words either.

## Tech context (for reference, not for the brief)
The real site is Next.js App Router + Tailwind + Drizzle/Neon Postgres, deployed on Vercel. The generator can output any framework — we'll port the ideas, not the code.
